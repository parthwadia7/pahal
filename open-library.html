The Community Open-Library

The Community Open-library Savitribai Phule and Fatima Sheikh Initiative of the PAHAL is a program designed for an urban slums community, targeting children and youth in particular, it seeks to provide them with an opportunity to access the treasures of knowledge and information by developing their reading & comprehension skills.

While urban Indians are aware of the unequal opportunities for children depending upon their socio-economic class, they are not always conscious of the dire situation in rural India. The disparity is the widest and most striking when it comes to access to education and occasions for formal learning. There are almost no opportunities for a village child, other than the village school, (which is unfortunately often non-functional), private schools are often just as bad, & sometimes worse as their teachers may not even be trained. Both these schools function without science laboratories & libraries, in effect, they also manage without toilets and sometimes even desks & chairs.

Objective

Our aim to create a space for learning, as opposed to teaching, the central point being a library around which we plan to develop activities.

The core objectives of the community library initiative are:

Provide children in rural areas an opportunity to access the treasures of knowledge, and inculcate the habit of reading at an early age

Create a network of village libraries that would serve as centers of learning and informal education in villages (especially for children, youth and women).

Engage with the village community and use the network of libraries to educate, inform and spread awareness on democratic rights and development issues.

With community participation, the Savitribai Phule & Fatima Sheikh Open Library will create libraries which will be managed by trained members of the slums. Once a cluster and settlement level of libraries are functioning, a village book fair and rural literary fest will be organized to discover, and we encourage the children coming to study in the library to connect with education. In the month of July, they also connect them with regular education. Encourage and nurture the creative talents of children and students.

PAHAL has been organizing Also organize story fair. Where we create opportunities to hear and narrate vibrant stories from different communities. By the way, most of the books in the books are stories related to the upper class environment.

One of the fun things about our library is that they bring along stories from their own surroundings.
